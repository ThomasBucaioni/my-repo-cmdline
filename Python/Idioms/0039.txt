Come over: to go to someone else's house. June and her sister will come over for lunch on sunday, we are having chicken pie
Run out: to not have enough of something. I cannot believe that we have run out of milk! just when i wanted to make a cup of tea
Count on: it means that you are able to depend on somebody or something. It doesn't matter what happens, you will always be able to count on
A sight for sore eyes: a way of saying that you are happy to see someone. I can't believe that i haven't seen you in a year! you are a sight for sore eyes
Weak in the knees: to be overcome by a strong feeling, usually desire. The man makes me weak in the knees when he kisses me
Never mind: to urge somebody not to worry about something. Never mind, i will do it myself
Say what: used when asking someone to repeat what they have said. They are going out tonight, even though we told them to stay at home. "say what!"
Point blank: to fire something at such close range that it cannot be missed. I asked the boy whether he thought that i was pretty, he said "no" point blank, without even trying to spare my feelings
Cheat sheet: a cheat sheet is a sheet containing information. John got caught using a cheat sheet during the test, he will be receiving a failing grade and will be in detention for the next three months
In a huff: to be upset or angry about something. June was very upset because her mother would not buy her a new pair of shoes, so she stormed out of the room in a huff
