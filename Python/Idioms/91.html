go the extra mile

Meaning: to make an extra effort

Example: When it comes to weaker students, the teacher goes the extra mile to help them understand. Read on

hang in there

Meaning: wait, be patient till things work out

Example: Our company is going through tough times, but we're hanging in there; things will surely work out. Read on

come out swinging

Meaning: be confrontational

Example: The government came out swinging against the oppositions charges of corruption during the debate. Read on

shoot from the hip

Meaning: speak directly or bluntly, without caring for consequences

Example: Even if you don't agree with your boss' ideas, don't shoot from the hip. It won't augur well for your growth. Read on

tar with the same brush

Meaning: to think that somebody has the same bad qualities as others in a similar surrounding

Example: Though the impression is that all government officials are corrupt, some of them are quite honest; its not fair to tar them with the same brush. Read on

go down in flames

Meaning: to fail spectacularly

Example: The company went down in flames after reports came out that it had been financing illegal activities. Read on

cut somebody some slack

Meaning: give additional freedom to someone

Example: I know he is not up to the mark right now but let's cut him some slack. He's new to this environment and needs time to adjust. Read on

leave no stone unturned

Meaning: do everything that is possible in order to achieve something

Example: The chief of security said that his team would leave no stone unturned in nabbing the miscreants. Read on

your guess is as good as mine

Meaning: I don't know

Example: If you want to know which one is the best of these movies, well, your guess is as good as mine. Read on

pull yourself together

Meaning: to calm down and behave normally after an upsetting event

Example: I know its difficult to get over your loss, but try to pull yourself together and get on with life. Read on
