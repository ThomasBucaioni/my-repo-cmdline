Flog a dead horse: waste energy on something that has no chance of succeeding. He keeps trying to get his manuscripts published, but i think he is flogging a dead horse
You are what you eat: if you eat good food you will be healthy, if you eat bad food you will be unhealthy. To stay healthy, it's best to keep off junk food. You are what you eat
Tongue in cheek: something said in humour, but with an act of being serious. The latest movie i watched was a tongue in cheek look at the way the media tends to over-hype certain pieces of news
Water under the bridge: past events that are not important anymore. We used to have big disagreements some years back, but that's all water under the bridge now. We get along fine
Pull leg: tease someone. Don't worry about what he said. He's just pulling your leg
Take a rain check: decline an offer that might be taken up later. I'll take a rain check on the party tonight, i have a lot of work to finish right now
Rule of thumb: a principle that is kept to. As a rule of thumb, i do not start a new project on fridays
Smell a rat: sense that something is not right. When he made that offer, i smelt a rat. It sounded too good to be true
Spitting image: look exactly like someone else. My sister is the spitting image of my mother
Until the cows come home: for a very long time. We can keep on arguing about this till the cows come home, but it won't solve anything
