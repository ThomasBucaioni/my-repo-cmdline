head and shoulders above

Meaning: significantly better than everybody else in the field

Example: I'm so proud of our boy, Johnny - he was head and shoulders above the rest of his class in his school project. Read on

bang head against wall

Meaning: bang head against a brick wall

Example: Trying to keep this place clean and tidy is like hitting my head against a brick wall. Read on

memory like a sieve

Meaning: poor memory

Example: I'm so sorry I forgot your birthday - I've got a memory like a sieve! Read on

like father like son

Meaning: the man/boy being talked about is very much like/exactly like his father 

Example: Joe's doing really well on the football team this season. He has nearly matched Joe senior's reputation for being a sharp shooter! I guess it's a case of like father, like son. Read on

if it’s the last thing I do

Meaning: indicates determination to achieve something

Example: I am going to get this house cleaned today if it's the last thing I do. Read on

not the sharpest tool in the shed

Meaning: someone who is not very clever, according to others

Example: Joe doesn't seem to understand that we're working to a different schedule now, since the workload has doubled.  He's not the sharpest tool in the shed. Read on

a trip to the sun

Meaning: something which is very unlikely

Example: My dream has always been to settle in Australia. But I know it's like a trip to the sun. I just have to make peace with this place. Read on

touch off

Meaning: to ignite a thought

Example: The presentation touched off a very important point and I would like to spend some time talking about it. Read on

eat like a horse

Meaning: to eat a lot

Example: He eats like a horse - there's never enough left for everyone else once he's been at the buffet. Read on

time flies

Meaning: for a particular phase in life to seem shorter than it really was

Example: Time flies when I go to play with friends. Read on
