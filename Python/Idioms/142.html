List of All Idioms

Page 142 of 147

as long as

Meaning: to put condition of time on an action

Example: The car will keep running as long as you take good care of it. Read on

as for

Meaning: to be regarding (something or someone)

Example: The meeting stands dissolved now. As for the agenda, I am happy to make the decisions all by myself. Read on

as far as

Meaning: to the extent of something

Example: As far as you are concerned, this chapter is over here. You are not to contact her again for anything. Read on

all of a sudden

all of a sudden Meaning: suddenly, without advance warning. Example: All of sudden it became dark and the rain started.

after all

after all Meaning: considering the fact that something happened, something that is usually assumed. Example: You don’t need to call him. After all, he never calls you.

account for

Meaning: to have to provide an explanation for (something)

Example: The horrible weather accounted for more than 20 percent absenteeism at work today. Read on

according to

Meaning: as mentioned by someone else either verbal or scriptural

Example: According to the news channel, this robbery took place in the night whereas the police are stating that it happened during the day. Read on

about to

Meaning: to be at the point where something was just going to be done

Example: I was about to come to you for advice when you called me. Read on

as a matter of fact

Meaning: to be or have done something in actuality

Example: As a matter of fact, I told her just yesterday about how they tease her child in school and how he is bullied by the bigger kids. Read on

avant-garde

Meaning: this phrase refers to artists whose work was ahead of their times

Example: This fair has many sculptures and paintings that are avant-garde. Read on
