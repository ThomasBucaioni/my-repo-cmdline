Break open: to cause something to open suddenly, especially from within. The violent storms shook the very foundation of the house and broke open the doors
Hang on: hold on or hold tightly. The child hung on to her mother and would not let go of her hands
What are you up to: another way to ask someone, what are you doing?. Seeing me going from room to room with no apparent method, my friend couldn't help but ask what i was up to
At the same time: used to refer to two or more things occurring together. My mother has the ability to make me laugh and cry at the same time
None of your business: used to tell someone not to get involved in whatever you have going on. My mother in law was not pleased when we politely told her that the honeymoon plan was none of her business
High up: to have an important standing in society. With the way he barked orders at the workers, everyone got the impression that he is high up in the company
All set: to refer a state of being ready for an event. After putting the luggage in the trunk, we were all set to go on our vacation
Pass away: die. Martha let us know that our grandfather passed away two nights ago
Stay up: not go to sleep. Mom made sure we stayed up to welcome our dad home after months of him being on military tour
Go after: to catch up to someone after they have gone. Leo has decided to go after his dreams with all that he has
