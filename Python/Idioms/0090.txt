Chip on shoulder: holding a grudge or grievance. He's always picking up fights with everyone. He seems to have a chip on his shoulder
Jump the gun: start something too soon or early, especially without thinking. The media jumped the gun and gave out their verdict of the scandal even before the investigation was complete
Over the moon: extremely pleased or happy. Judy was over the moon when tim proposed to her
Hit the books: to study very hard. I have an exam tomorrow, i need to hit the books tonight
Stone’s throw: a short distance. His workplace is a stone's throw away from his home
Let the chips fall where they may: let something happen, no matter what happens after that. Honesty demands that you tell the truth about what happened, and let the chips fall where they may
Tickled pink: excited and happy. His wife was tickled pink when he sent her flowers and gifts at work for no reason
Blow away: impress someone very much. This new has has such an interesting story and an amazing ending that it just blew me away
Couldn’t care less: total lack of interest in something. I have heard that my ex is going through another break-up, but i couldn't care less
Sell someone out: to betray someone. The company had put a lot of trust on him, but he sold them out by leaking confidential information to the competitors
