All greek to me: used to convey that you cannot understand what is being said or written. Don't try to explain the technicalities of how this machine works; it would be all greek to me
All bark and no bite: threatening, aggressive, but not willing to engage in a fight. I heard he has threatened you with dire consequences if you don not stop that construction. Don't worry, he is all bark and no bite
Toss-up: a situation where the result is unclear and can go either way. They had a tough time selecting the team for the big match. In the end, it was a toss-up between having the most experienced players and having a youthful team full of raw energy
A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush: what you already have is more valuable than the prospect to have something greater. You may not like your job, but don't quit merely on the hope of finding a better one. A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush
Ask out: invite someone to go out, especially on a date. When he finally asked her out, she readily accepted, and very soon they were dating on a regular basis
End of story: there is  nothing more to add to the matter under discussion. I did not invite her because i did not want her to come to my party. End of story
Joined at the hip: two people who are inseparable. Chris and jenny have been joined at the hip since they started dating each other a year ago. You would never see one without the other
Dog days: hot, sultry days. During the dog days of summer, people prefer to remain indoors and go out as little as possible
Hard cheese: bad luck, tough luck. The construction workers have a tough time out there, working continuously in the sweltering heat. It's really hard cheese for them
Salad days: the time of youth, innocence and inexperience. They had met in their salad days and had fallen in love. Naturally, the union did not last long and they separated a few years later
