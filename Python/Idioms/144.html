axe to grind

Meaning: have a private or selfish reason for doing something

Example: He should not become the chairman of the committee as he has too many axes of his own to grind. Read on

Adam’s ale

Meaning: water

Example: It is blistering hot outside, I could really do with an iced cold glass of Adam's ale. Read on

keep at arm’s length

Meaning: keep distance from something or somebody

Example: I like to keep my office colleagues at arm's length outside of office. I don't want them in my personal life. Read on

in the ascendant

Meaning: to become more powerful

Example: The Jones' have been in the ascendant ever since the 1800's. They are now the most influential family in the United Kingdom, after the Queen's own of course. Read on

have been around

have been around Meaning: have existed; have acquired a lot of worldly experience. Example: There is nothing new about laptops – they’ve been around for years.

armed to the teeth

Meaning: heavily and formidably armed, usually with deadly weapons

Example: The alleged tax-defaulter was ready for all their questions, and armed to the teeth with proof of his innocence. Read on

as and when

Meaning: at the time when (used to refer to an uncertain future event)

Example: Most people in this city don't own a car - they just rent one as and when they need it. Read on

up in arms

Meaning: angry

Example: My mom was up in arms about the new salary cuts rumoured to be put in place. Read on

long arm of the law

Meaning: the extensive influence of the authorities

Example: It is not easy for criminals to escape the long arm of the law in a developed country like United Kingdom. Read on

seal of approval

Meaning: an official sign of approval

Example: The government finally agreed to give the new pension policy its seal of approval. Read on
