Stands to reason: to reach a logical conclusion. The company stands to reason that the project will take at least another six months to conclude given that none of the managers have a better grip on the work
Up to no good: to have an evil plan. The children can be up to no good cooped up in that attic together for two hours. You must go up and check
Zip it: to ask someone to shut up. The teacher had asked the class to zip it but today everybody seemed to want to talk a lot. So she punished the entire class
In vain: without success. It seems as if the merger will go through and all our hard work has been in vain
Sleep tight: an affectionate way of wishing someone a good night's sleep. I am going to bed now. Good night, sleep tight
On your mark: absolutely accurate. Runners, please get ready. On your mark, get set, go!
Too close to call: a margin that is too close to determine a winner. There are only a few minutes left in the match and it is too close to call
The devil is beating his wife: raining whereas the sun is shining. Today, the devil is beating his wife in our city - it's raining cats and dog and sun is also shining
When hell freezes over: impolite way of saying "it will never happen". I guess, she will forgive you when hell freezes over
All that glitters is not gold: the shining outer look of something is not a consistent sign of its real character. My grandmother advised me to be careful about making new friends because all that glitters is not gold
